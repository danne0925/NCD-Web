What Are Non-Communicable Diseases?

Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs) are long-lasting medical conditions that are not spread from person to person. They develop slowly and are often the result of genetic, physiological, environmental, and behavioral factors.

Types of NCDs

  • Cardiovascular Diseases: Heart attacks, strokes
  • Cancers: Lung, breast, colon, and other forms
  • Chronic Respiratory Diseases: Asthma, COPD
  • Diabetes: Type 1 and 2
  • Mental Health Disorders: Depression, anxiety, Alzheimerâ€™s

Causes of NCDs

  • Unhealthy diet and physical inactivity
  • Tobacco use and harmful alcohol consumption
  • Air pollution and exposure to harmful chemicals
  • Stress and poor mental health
  • Genetic and hereditary factors

Effects of NCDs

  • Reduced quality of life and chronic disability
  • High healthcare costs and economic burden
  • Premature death if left untreated
  • Psychological distress for individuals and families

Prevention & Management

  • Eat a balanced, nutritious diet
  • Engage in daily physical activity
  • Avoid smoking and limit alcohol intake
  • Manage stress and mental health
  • Regular health screenings and early diagnosis

Interesting Facts

  • NCDs cause 41 million deaths annually (71% of all deaths globally)
  • 80% of NCDs are preventable through lifestyle changes
  • Low-income countries bear a disproportionate burden
